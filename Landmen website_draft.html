The bottleneck

You don't have a demand problem. You have a capacity problem.

01 — Courthouse drag

Every project waits on a runner in a records room.

Whether it's a county that still uses microfiche or one with a clunky online portal, the first two weeks of every project are spent pulling, scanning, and transcribing documents a human has already read a thousand times.

02 — Examiner tax

Your best people spend 60% of their day on work a junior could do.

Senior examiners who should be resolving gaps, flagging risk, and talking to clients are instead retyping grantor/grantee data, formatting runsheets, and reconciling parcel IDs across systems.

03 — Can't scale to win

Renewables and pipeline RFPs need 500 tracts in 30 days.

The work is there. Solar, wind, transmission, midstream. They all need massive land packages, fast. But bidding means promising turnarounds you can't staff for, or walking away from seven-figure contracts.

04 — Fixed-price bleed

One messy county can flip a project from profit to loss.

You price the bid on average effort. Then you hit a county with 80 years of handwritten deeds, a curative nightmare, or a last-minute client scope change, and there's nowhere for the overrun to go except your margin.

05 — Knowledge walks out

The examiner who "just knows" that county is 64 and retiring.

Three decades of institutional knowledge about oddball conveyances, local naming conventions, and which title companies run clean. None of it is written down. When they leave, you start over.

06 — QC is another bottleneck

Reports go out with errors, or sit in review for a week.

Either you ship fast and eat client pushback on missed instruments, or you bottleneck every deliverable through one senior reviewer. Both options cost you reputation and revenue.

How it works

You keep your workflow. We plug into it.

We don't replace your case management system, your templates, or your team's process. We shadow what you already do, and take the repetitive steps off the critical path.

Week 1

We learn your workflow.

Our team sits with your senior examiners for two days. We map your runsheet format, your report templates, your QC checkpoints, and the three or four counties where you do the most work.

Week 2

We build your pipeline.

We configure the automation to your exact outputs, not a generic template. Your first workflow goes live on real client work, running alongside your team as a backstop, not a replacement.

Weeks 3–4

We tune and expand.

Your team flags anything the draft misses; we adjust. By end of month, Basinfoundry is handling 80%+ of the retrieval and drafting work for your most common project type.

Ongoing

We stay on the phone.

You have a named engineer on your account. New county, new client template, weird curative case: you call, we ship. This is a service, not a login.
