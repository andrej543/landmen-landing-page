The bottleneck

The work is there. The timelines are the problem.

01 — The RFP math doesn't work

A 400-tract solar site in 45 days requires a bench you don't have.

Either you hire a dozen temporary examiners and take the culture hit, or you decline seven-figure revenue. Neither option is how you build a firm.

02 — Surface title at scale

Every single tract needs a quiet chain.

Unlike oil and gas, renewables need clean surface title on every parcel. Hundreds of chains, every one with ROW clearances, mineral severance flags, and encumbrance review. The volume is the problem.

03 — Mineral severance everywhere

One old 1952 mineral reservation can kill a site.

Developers need to know which tracts have severed minerals and what surface-use rights those mineral owners retained. Missing it on even one parcel means the project footprint moves, or the interconnect queue slot gets forfeited.

04 — Fixed developer deadlines

PPA dates and tax equity closings don't slip.

Developer deadlines are driven by offtake agreements and investment tax credit windows. If you miss the date by two weeks, the project slips a year and the developer remembers which land firm caused it.

05 — Clients want a portal, not a PDF

Renewables teams expect parcel-level status, live.

Your oil and gas clients accepted a weekly status email. Renewables project managers want a dashboard with every tract, every owner, every curative item, and they want it updated in real time.

06 — Option and lease expirations

A 12-month option clock starts the day you sign.

Site control documents expire. Notice of commencement deadlines trigger. Without parcel-level tracking, firms let options lapse on tracts they need and re-negotiate from a weaker position, if they can negotiate at all.
